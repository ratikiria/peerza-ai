Why Solana, not anything else

Trader reputation needs Solana economics.

We didn't pick Solana for the brand. We picked it because the reputation primitive only works at Solana's fee curve and composability model. Remove Solana and the product becomes a walled garden. Keep it, and it becomes a Solana-native primitive other apps build on.

01 · Fees

Per-action minting is viable.

Every duel won, lesson completed, winning trade — can mint a cNFT proof. Sub-cent fees + Bubblegum make this economically real. On L2s it's $0.20–$2 per action. That kills the unit economics before launch.

02 · Composability

Identity is portable by default.

Trader score is a public account on Solana. Any other Solana app — DEX, lending market, DAO, prediction pool — reads it directly. No SDK to integrate, no API key to issue. Composability is what makes reputation a primitive instead of a feature.

03 · Rails

Global creator payouts work.

USDC tipping + Pro subscription via Solana Pay. We pay a creator in Lagos and another in Tbilisi without negotiating Stripe coverage country by country. Stablecoin rails are the only ones that scale to where Peerza's audience actually lives.

Acid test: if you removed Solana from Peerza, you'd have another walled-garden trader-Twitter. Solana is what makes the reputation portable, the economics viable, and the payouts global. The chain isn't a logo — it's load-bearing.
The Primitive

Trader score, written to Solana. Read by anyone.

Other consumer apps stop at the walled garden. Peerza's trader score is a public Solana account — any other program reads it directly, no SDK, no API key, no gatekeeping. That's what turns a feature into a primitive.
